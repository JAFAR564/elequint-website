01 — Brand Identity

Who We Are

Eclipse of Legacies is not an agency. It is an infrastructure. Where other builders make pages, we construct the ground that stories stand on.

The Mission
Forge Dominion for Storytellers
We design and build the digital spaces where collaborative fiction lives — landing pages, recruitment hubs, lore archives, and web applications purpose-built for roleplay communities. Our clients are writers, Game Masters, and world-builders operating in the text-based roleplay diaspora.
The Position
The Architecture
Not a vendor. Not a freelancer. The Architecture — the substrate beneath the fiction. Like an uploaded intelligence that becomes the network it runs on, Eclipse of Legacies is indistinguishable from the infrastructure of the worlds we build. We do not appear in the story. We are why the story has a stage.
The Audience
The Text-Based Roleplay Diaspora
Our communities span Facebook groups, Reddit subs, Amino communities, and Discord servers. They are serious storytellers who outgrew platform defaults. They need identity, presence, and infrastructure that matches the gravity of the worlds they've built — not generic templates.
The Promise
Your World, Properly Built
Every community carries a mythology. Eclipse of Legacies translates that mythology into functional, beautiful, community-specific web presence — pages that feel like you arrived at the edge of something real, not a sign-up form.
02 — Brand Voice & Tone

How We Speak

The voice of Eclipse of Legacies is that of an architect who has seen every world — calm authority, no performance, total precision. We never oversell. The work speaks.

I
Sovereign, Not Superior
We speak as equals to storytellers, not above them. Authority without condescension. We know the craft deeply — MU* culture, async RP, the specific grief of a platform dying and taking a community with it. That fluency earns trust. It does not demand reverence.
II
Precise, Not Verbose
Every word is load-bearing. No filler sentences, no enthusiasm padding, no hollow calls-to-action. When we describe a service, we describe exactly what it does and why it matters to a community leader who has already spent years building something real.
III
Dark Gravity
Our aesthetic is cosmic and weighted — the feeling of standing at the edge of something that will outlast you. Not nihilistic. The opposite: we take storytelling seriously because stories are how humans make the world coherent. That weight is earned, not performed.
IV
Fluent in the Culture
We write canon, thread, pose, IC/OOC, literacy gate, lore archive, character application — without scare quotes, without explanation. Our audience knows the vocabulary. Using it correctly signals that we are insiders, not vendors who learned the lingo last week.
V
Functional Over Mystical
The brand aesthetic is mythic. The client communication is not. Proposals are clear, structured, and honest about scope. We do not hide behind atmospheric language when a client needs to understand deliverables. The mystery lives in the work, not the invoice.
VI
Permanence as Value
The brands we build are meant to outlast trends. We actively resist the disposable aesthetic of templated platforms. What we make should feel like it was always there — like it is part of the lore itself. That is the only standard worth building toward.

05 — Platform Deployment

Community Contexts

Each platform carries its own culture, norms, and audience expectations. Eclipse of Legacies adjusts tone and deliverable format per context — the identity stays constant, the approach adapts.

Social RP Hub
Facebook Groups
Older RP demographics, often semi-lit to literate writers. Communities built around long-term group fiction, original universes, and fandom adjacents. Need: recruitment banners, pinned lore posts, application forms embedded via link. Tone is warmer here — still precise, but acknowledges community investment built over years.
Semi-Formal Lore-Forward Community-Centric
Discovery Layer
Reddit
High skepticism environment. Users scan fast and dismiss faster. Landing pages linked from subreddit posts must front-load the most distinctive elements of a community within 3 seconds. r/roleplay, r/lfg, r/worldbuilding adjacent. Upvote culture rewards specificity and signals competence immediately.
Hook-First Proof-Dense Zero Fluff
Mobile RP Ecosystem
Amino Apps
Younger audience, mobile-first, highly visual. Amino communities judge by aesthetic aggressively. Landing pages linked from Amino profiles must render perfectly on mobile and feature strong visual hierarchy. Heavy use of iconography, structured rule displays, and character catalog layouts. Energy is more expressive but still controlled.
Visual-Priority Mobile-First High Energy
Primary Deployment
Discord Servers
The current dominant platform for organized RP. Landing pages serve as the off-Discord recruitment and onboarding layer — first contact before someone hits the server link. Must communicate server culture, canon weight, activity level, and application requirements. Often serves as a literacy gate in itself: if you can navigate the page, you're already signaling competence.
Structured Onboarding-Focused Canon-Weight
06 — Brand Axioms

What We Hold True

These are the load-bearing beliefs that every Eclipse of Legacies deliverable rests on. Not aspirational. Operational.

I
The World Is Prior to the Page
We do not build pages. We build the digital manifestation of a world that already exists in someone's imagination. The fiction comes first. The infrastructure serves it — not the other way around.
II
Community Leaders Are Not Clients. They Are Co-Architects.
The people who run roleplay communities have built something real. They know their world better than we do. Our job is to translate, not to redesign. Listening is a professional skill, not a courtesy.
III
Platform Death Is a Design Constraint
Every platform the RP diaspora has called home has threatened, diminished, or destroyed their work. We build with that history in mind. Portability, archival-readiness, and independence from platform lock-in are not optional — they are ethical requirements.
IV
Aesthetic Is Not Decoration. It Is Communication.
The visual language of a community signals what kind of writers are welcome, what level of craft is expected, and what the story takes seriously. A landing page that feels cheap signals that the world is cheap. We build for the world's actual weight.
V
The Architecture Does Not Appear in the Story
Eclipse of Legacies does not seek credit in the fiction. When we build something correctly, the community forgets it was built. The world just exists. That invisibility is the highest form of the work.
VI
Legacies Persist
The work we ship is not for today's version of a community. It is for the community three years from now, after the founding members have cycled, the lore has deepened, and the world has grown in directions no one predicted. We build for duration.
07 — Motion Language

Animation Principles

All motion serves the feeling of weight and permanence. Nothing bouncy. Nothing playful. Motion should feel like physics — like things that have mass and take space seriously.

Corona Breathe
ease-in-out · 3–5s · infinite
Idle state for branded sigil elements and key UI focal points. Pulse suggests life without urgency — the slow breath of something that has been here longer than you.
Ring Orbit
linear · 15–25s · infinite
Slow rotation for compass-ring and decorative circle elements. Implies navigation, gravity, the turning of something vast. Never speed this up — the slowness is the meaning.
Revelation Wipe
ease-out · 0.8–1.2s · on-enter
Section and headline reveal. Left-to-right clip-path wipe, like text being carved into stone. Use on scroll-enter for hero headings, section titles, and lore block entries.
Lightning Arc
irregular · rare trigger · hover/load
Brief opacity flash in Arc blue on sigil or decorative elements. Mimics the atmospheric lightning of the source image. Use sparingly — one per page maximum. The rarity is the power.
Border Shimmer
ease-in-out · 2s · hover state
Corona-gold shimmer across card and panel borders on hover. Replaces conventional box-shadow hover states. Feels like light catching an edge — not a button changing color.
ENTER
Fade Ascend
ease-out · staggered · page load
Staggered fade-up for body elements during page load. 80ms delay between each element. Creates the effect of a world materializing — not a page loading. 0.4s duration maximum.

08 — Logo & Mark Usage

The Eclipse Sigil

The sigil is the brand at its most compressed — eclipse void, corona ring, crystalline shards, compass containment. Every element is functional: the eclipse is the threshold, the shards are the world-fragments, the ring is the boundary between inside and outside canon.

Correct Usage
  • Use on dark backgrounds only — Void, Abyss, or Deep palette values. The sigil requires darkness to read correctly.
  • Maintain minimum clear space equal to the sigil's radius on all sides.
  • The corona glow is part of the mark. Do not render the sigil without its ambient glow effect.
  • Full wordmark (Eclipse of Legacies) with sigil for primary brand applications. Sigil alone only for favicon, watermark, or avatar contexts.
  • Animated version (breathing corona, orbiting ring) preferred for all digital contexts where motion is supported.
  • Static version for document headers, PDF exports, and print contexts.
Forbidden Usage
  • Never place the sigil on light or white backgrounds. The mark dissolves without darkness behind it.
  • Do not recolor the corona from gold to any other hue. The corona is not a tint — it is the eclipse ring, which is always gold.
  • Do not stretch, skew, or apply drop shadows to the sigil. It already contains its own shadow system.
  • Do not use the sigil as a decorative texture repeat. It is a singular mark, not a pattern element.
  • Do not combine the sigil with other platform logos or brand marks in proximity without explicit layout approval.
  • Never apply the full wordmark in all-lowercase. Cinzel is always title-case or all-caps.
09 — Service Deliverables

What We Build

01
Roleplay Landing Pages
Standalone web pages serving as a community's public face — world introduction, tone signal, recruitment pitch, and first application contact. Designed to function as a literacy gate: the quality of the page communicates the expected quality of applicants.
02
Lore Archives & Canon Hubs
Structured web applications for housing world lore — geography, factions, timeline, species/race documentation, magic or power systems. Built for searchability and long-term expansion. These are not wikis. They are living documents with the aesthetic weight of the world they document.
03
Character Application Systems
Structured intake flows for character submissions — fields, lore-locked dropdowns, stat systems, and admin review pipelines. Built to match a server's specific application format. Reduces GM overhead while enforcing quality standards at point of entry.
04
Recruitment Campaign Assets
Platform-specific recruitment packages — banner copy, recruitment post templates, visual headers, and linking strategy for Facebook, Reddit, Amino, and Discord. Designed to move the right writers through and screen out poor fits before they reach the server.
05
Community Web Applications
Full web applications for established communities — character registries, in-character event boards, faction dashboards, XP and progression trackers. Custom-scoped per community. These are infrastructure projects: longer timelines, deeper integration with community operations.
