03 — Platform Deployment

Where We Deploy

Each platform carries its own culture, norms, and audience expectations. The identity stays constant. The approach adapts.

Social RP Hub
Facebook Groups
Older RP demographics, often semi-lit to literate writers. Long-term group fiction, original universes, fandom adjacents. Need: recruitment banners, pinned lore posts, application forms. Tone is warmer here — still precise, but acknowledges community investment built over years.
Semi-Formal Lore-Forward Community-Centric
Discovery Layer
Reddit
High skepticism environment. Users scan fast and dismiss faster. Landing pages must front-load the most distinctive elements within 3 seconds. r/roleplay, r/lfg, r/worldbuilding adjacent. Upvote culture rewards specificity and signals competence immediately.
Hook-First Proof-Dense Zero Fluff
Mobile RP Ecosystem
Amino Apps
Younger audience, mobile-first, highly visual. Amino communities judge by aesthetic aggressively. Pages must render perfectly on mobile with strong visual hierarchy. Heavy iconography, structured rule displays, character catalog layouts.
Visual-Priority Mobile-First High Energy
Primary Deployment
Discord Servers
The current dominant platform for organized RP. Landing pages serve as off-Discord recruitment and onboarding — first contact before the server link. Must communicate server culture, canon weight, activity level, and application requirements.
Structured Onboarding-Focused Canon-Weight
04 — How It Works

The Commission Process

Four stages from world to web. Structured, transparent, and built around your canon — not a template workflow.

Step 01
World Intake
You walk us through your world. Canon, culture, current platform problems, target audience, tone. We listen. We ask the right questions. We learn the lore before we write a single line of code.
Step 02
Architecture Draft
We deliver a structural proposal — layout, sections, information hierarchy, design direction. No mockups yet. Just the ground plan. You approve the bones before we build the walls.
Step 03
Build & Refine
Full build delivered as a working page. Two rounds of revision included — this is where your world's details come in. Specific colors, exact copy, faction-specific imagery, application form fields.
Step 04
Deploy & Handoff
Final files delivered with hosting guidance. You own everything. No platform lock-in. Documentation included so your admin team can update content without coming back to us for every edit.
05 — What We Hold True

Brand Axioms

These are the load-bearing beliefs every Eclipse of Legacies deliverable rests on. Not aspirational. Operational.

I
The World Is Prior to the Page
We do not build pages. We build the digital manifestation of a world that already exists in someone's imagination. The fiction comes first. The infrastructure serves it — not the other way around.
II
Co-Architects, Not Clients
The people who run roleplay communities have built something real. They know their world better than we do. Our job is to translate, not to redesign. Listening is a professional skill, not a courtesy.
III
Platform Death Is a Design Constraint
Every platform the RP diaspora has called home has threatened or destroyed their work. We build with that history in mind. Portability and archival-readiness are not optional — they are ethical requirements.
IV
Aesthetic Is Communication
The visual language of a community signals what kind of writers are welcome. A landing page that feels cheap signals the world is cheap. We build for the world's actual weight.
V
The Architecture Disappears
When we build something correctly, the community forgets it was built. The world just exists. That invisibility is the highest form of the work. Eclipse of Legacies does not seek credit in the fiction.
VI
Legacies Persist
The work we ship is not for today's version of a community. It is for the community three years from now, after the lore has deepened and the world has grown in directions no one predicted. We build for duration.
