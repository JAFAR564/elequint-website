The Setting

The Realm of Caldenmere

Three centuries after the Veilshatter sundered the sky and fractured the continent's aether-lines, Caldenmere is a world of careful alliances and dangerous ambitions. Six sovereign factions rose from the ruins — each inheriting a fragment of the old world's power, each convinced their claim to the broken throne is the only legitimate one.

This is not a world of simple evil. Every faction has survived impossible odds. Every ruler has made choices they cannot take back. Every treaty hides a clause that could shatter it. Writers here don't just inhabit a world — they shape its politics, its history, and its future.

The Factions

Six Peoples. Six Philosophies of Power.

Each faction offers a distinct playstyle, political structure, and lore depth. Choose your allegiance — or play the fractures between them.

I
Faction I · The Gilded Realm
The Auric Dominion

A mercantile empire built on the trade of aether-crystals and political marriages. The Dominion controls the continent's three great overland routes and has turned wealth into a form of sovereignty. Their motto: "Gold does not rust."

II
Faction II · The Ancient Canopy
The Thornveil Court

A druidic sovereignty that predates the Fracture by four thousand years. The Court does not govern — it tends. Its druids speak for the living world itself, and their word carries the authority of a continent's oldest memory.

Circle of Twelve Wardens Living Archive (The Deep Root) Fauna-Bond Legions Ageless Counsel
III
Faction III · The Iron State
The Covenant of Tempered Steel

A forge-republic governed by a Senate of Guildmasters and elected Strategoi. The Covenant believes that the only true sovereignty is the one you can defend. They are the continent's finest military engineers and its most principled lawmakers.

Senate of Forty-Eight Guilds Strategos Electoral System Warmachine Vanguard Codex of Tempered Law
IV
Faction IV · The Neutral Ground
The Ashen Conclave

A sovereign order of scholars who occupy the continent's geographic center — and its political center too. The Conclave sells knowledge to all factions and allegiance to none. They are the world's memory, its archives, and its most dangerous neutral party.

Grand Archivist Oligarchy The Unburning Library Treaty Notary Authority Forbidden Research Vaults
V
Faction V · The Maritime League
The Tidecrown Collective

A federation of seventeen island-cities and coastal city-states united under a rotating Admiralty. The Collective controls the seas, the trade winds, and the world's most lucrative information networks. Their fleets have never lost a blockade.

Rotating Admiralty Council Seventeen Sovereign Ports The Windwhisper Network Tidal Compact Law
VI
Faction VI · The Undying Realm
The Hollow Throne

An undead sovereignty in the continent's northeastern wastes — feared, misunderstood, and far more complex than the living give them credit for. The Hollow Throne remembers every war. Their Lich-King has witnessed three centuries of other factions' mistakes firsthand.
